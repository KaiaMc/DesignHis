JAPAN

Japanese comics (commonly referred to as manga), began to take prominence in the country around the 1950’s, following the introduction of American comics during WWII. Since then, manga has only grown as an industry, expanding outside of the country and influencing the global comic industry with its unique aesthetic styles.

Astro Boy (Mighty Atom)

One of the most well-known classic manga series, Astro Boy has sold 100 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best selling manga series of all time. Astro Boy is credited as being a turning point for manga and anime, and even influencing comics worldwide, laying foundations for some beloved American media such as The Lion King.
“...One explanation for the popularity of comics in Japan, however, is that Japan had Osamu Tezuka, whereas other nations did not. Without Dr. Tezuka, the postwar explosion in comics in Japan would have been inconceivable,” - John Sisson, Biological Sciences Research Librarian

Fist of the North Star

Fist of the North Star sits among Astro Boy as being one of the best selling manga series of all time, however this property specifically is also credited as one of the highest-grossing media franchises. Fist of the North star directly influenced the creation of many well known franchises (such as the Mortal Kombat video game series, “Jojo’s Bizarre Adventure”, and “Berserk”, just to name a few notable titles), along with inspiring many with its highly unique and distinct artistic style.
